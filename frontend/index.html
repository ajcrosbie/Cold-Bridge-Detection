Before you upload

  • Tip 1: Stand roughly 2m away from the wall and keep the camera as straight-on as possible.
  • Tip 2: Ensure the heating has been on inside to create a strong temperature difference (ΔT) between inside and outside.
  • Tip 3: Avoid direct sunlight on external walls, mixed indoor/outdoor framing and watch out for reflective surfaces which can confuse the thermal camera.
  • Tip 4: Try to include the full wall section you want analysed.
  • Format instructions: Create a separate folder for each suspected cold bridge (e.g., "Kitchen Window", "Living Room Wall").
  • Place your thermal photos into their respective folders, then compress all those folders into a single ZIP file.
  • Alternatively, drop loose photos here.

What do these values mean?

  • Severity: A metric to gauge how bad the pictured cold bridge is. Designed by us to be easily understandable for non-expert users. It is based on psi-value.
  • Psi-value: measures linear thermal bridging. This is the extra heat escaping through building junctions. Expressed in W/mK, lower values indicate better thermal performance, reduced energy loss, and less risk of condensation. A lower psi-value shows better insulation.
  • Temperature factor/Frsi: A dimensionless ratio used to evaluate the risk of condensation and mould growth on a surface. It measures how warm an internal surface remains relative to the indoor and outdoor air temperatures. A higher value indicates lower condensation risk and is better.
  • Sensitivity: A value indicating how sensitive a cold bridge is to outside temperature changes. A higher value indicates that a cold bridge is more sensitive. This is not necessarily better or worse and its interpretation instead depends on the environment your building is located in.
